## **`Parent Training: Frequently Asked Questions`**

### **`How much time will I need to commit each week?`**

`We understand your schedule is busy. Most families participate in 1–2 hours of collaborative training per month or bi-weekly, depending on your child’s specific plan. Our goal is to integrate these strategies into the things you’re already doing—like mealtime or play—so it doesn't feel like "extra" work.`

### **`Do I need to be a "teacher" for this to work?`**

`Not at all. You are the parent, and that is your most important role. We don't expect you to become a therapist; we simply provide you with a "toolbox" of small, effective strategies to help manage behaviors and encourage communication in your natural daily routines.`

### **`Can both parents or caregivers participate?`**

`Absolutely. In fact, we encourage it! Consistency is the "bridge" to success. We welcome moms, dads, grandparents, or any primary caregivers to join our sessions so that everyone in the child’s world is on the same page.`

### **`Is this covered by my insurance?`**

`In most cases, yes. In Ohio and New Jersey, many insurance providers recognize that parent training is a vital component of successful ABA therapy and include it in their coverage. We will verify this for you during the intake process so there are no surprises.`
