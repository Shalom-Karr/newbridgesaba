Understanding ABA

ABA (Applied Behavior Analysis) is a scientifically-validated approach to understanding and changing behavior. It focuses on increasing desired behaviors (like communication and social skills) and reducing problematic behaviors by using positive reinforcement strategies tailored to the individual.
Our services are specifically designed for individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and other developmental disabilities. We serve children and young adults from early intervention ages through age 21.

The New Bridges Model

Consistency is the foundation of progress. When a child learns a skill in school but cannot apply it at home, their progress is stalled. By sending the same therapist to both settings, we ensure that skills generalize, routines stay predictable, and the child feels supported 100% of the time.
Therapy goals are determined through a collaborative process. Our Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs) conduct a thorough assessment of your child’s strengths and needs, then work with parents and school staff to build a plan that prioritizes the most impactful skills for the child’s independence.

Insurance & Locations

Yes! We are proud to serve the entire state of Ohio. We accept various insurance plans and private pay options. Our intake team will handle the verification process with your provider to confirm your coverage details before therapy begins.
To start the process, we generally require basic demographic information, a copy of the child’s ASD diagnosis from a qualified professional, insurance card details, and any current IEP or school documentation you may have.

Staff Qualifications

Our staff are highly trained professionals. Every clinical team consists of Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs) and Registered Behavior Technicians (RBTs). They receive ongoing supervision, data-driven training, and updates on clinical best practices to ensure your child receives the highest level of care.
